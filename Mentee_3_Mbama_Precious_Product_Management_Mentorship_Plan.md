## PROGRAMME OVERVIEW

Welcome to your 5-week intensive Product Management mentorship, Precious! This programme is designed to take you from foundational understanding to confident, portfolio-ready Product Manager. Product Management sits at the intersection of business, technology, and user experience — and the best PMs are curious, empathetic, data-informed, and decisive. Over the next 5 weeks, you'll learn how the world's best product teams at companies like Airbnb, Spotify, Notion, and Slack think, build, and ship products that millions love. Each week is packed with real-world case studies, hands-on projects, the latest tools, and resources from the best minds in the industry. By the end, you'll have a professional portfolio of PM deliverables that demonstrates real-world competence.

**Your North Star for this programme:** *Learn to identify real user problems, define the right solutions, and rally teams to build products people can't live without.*

### Case Study 1: Spotify — Discover Weekly and the Power of User Data
- Study how Spotify built Discover Weekly, one of the most beloved product features in music streaming history. It launched in 2015 and within weeks had 40 million users.
  - How did Spotify's product team identify the user need for music discovery?
  - What data signals and user research informed the feature?
  - How did they test and iterate before the full launch?
  - What role did the squad model (autonomous cross-functional teams) play?

### Case Study 2: Duolingo — Gamification as Product Strategy

  - How did they use gamification (streaks, leaderboards, hearts) to drive retention?
  - What is Duolingo's North Star Metric (Daily Active Learners) and how does every feature connect to it?
  - How did they expand from language learning to math, music, and more?

- Sources: Duolingo's S-1 filing, Lenny's Newsletter deep-dive on Duolingo, Jorge Mazal's (former Duolingo PM) talks on YouTube

### Frameworks to Master
- **RICE Prioritisation** (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) — Learn to score and rank features objectively
- **MoSCoW Method** (Must Have, Should Have, Could Have, Won't Have) — For sprint-level prioritisation
- **Kano Model** — Understand the difference between Basic, Performance, and Delight features
- **Now-Next-Later Roadmap** — The modern alternative to timeline-based roadmaps (invented by Janna Bastow of ProdPad)
- **OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)** — How Google, Spotify, and the best product teams set goals. Read "Measure What Matters" by John Doerr (Chapters 1-5)
- **Product Vision Board** — Roman Pichler's framework for connecting vision to strategy
- **Value vs Effort Matrix** — The simplest prioritisation tool for quick decisions

### Real-World Problem to Solve
- You're the PM for the telemedicine app. Your top priority feature from Week 3 needs to be built. Write a complete PRD for it including:

  - **Goal & Success Metrics**: What does success look like? Define 2-3 measurable KPIs.

### Case Study 1: Duolingo's Metrics Machine — How Data Drives Everything
- Deep-dive into how Duolingo uses product analytics to drive every decision:

  - Their approach to the "Hook Model" — notifications, streaks, leaderboards, and hearts

### Case Study 2: WhatsApp — The Art of the Simple Product Launch
- Study how WhatsApp grew to 2 billion users with one of the simplest product strategies in tech history:

  - Their famous "No Ads, No Games, No Gimmicks" manifesto
  - How did they measure success? (Messages sent per day, not revenue)
  - The $19B acquisition by Facebook — what product metrics justified that price?
- Sources: WhatsApp's original blog posts, David Rowan's "Non-Bullshit Innovation" chapter on WhatsApp, TechCrunch coverage
- **Write a 1-page analysis:** What does WhatsApp teach you about the power of simplicity in product management and choosing the right metrics?
